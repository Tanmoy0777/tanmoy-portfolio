🔬 Deep Analysis, Findings & Business Impact
Data-driven insights that shaped strategy, optimised spend, and drove measurable pipeline growth at NetCom Learning
📈
Organic Search: The Hidden Revenue Engine
Organic search drives 34% of total MQLs but contributes 41% of closed revenue — a $1,380 revenue-per-MQL rate, the highest of any channel. Despite generating fewer raw leads than LinkedIn paid campaigns, organic leads had a 28% conversion rate vs 22% for paid, and an average deal size 18% larger. This insight directly triggered a content-first SEO investment recommendation that increased organic spend allocation by 15%.
$1,380 rev/MQL · Highest ROI channel
⚠️
MQL→SQL: The Biggest Funnel Leak (75.3% Drop)
Analysis revealed that 75.3% of MQLs were never converting to SQLs — the largest single drop-off in the funnel. Root cause investigation using cohort analysis and CRM activity data showed two factors: (1) SDR team capacity was being overwhelmed by low-quality leads from broad paid campaigns, and (2) Marketo lead scoring thresholds were too permissive, allowing cold prospects to reach the sales queue. Recommendation: raise MQL threshold score from 40 to 55, add "budget confirmed" as a mandatory gate. Result: 24.7% MQL→SQL conversion rate — up 3.2pp year-over-year.
75.3% drop-off identified & addressed
🎯
ABM Launch Correlated with 28% Pipeline Spike
In Q3, the data revealed a 28% month-over-month pipeline spike that coincided exactly with the launch of an Account-Based Marketing (ABM) campaign targeting enterprise accounts by job title and company size. Prior to ABM, enterprise segment was only 38% of pipeline despite having the highest close rate (34%). Post-ABM, enterprise grew to 50% of pipeline within 60 days. This data point made the business case for doubling ABM investment in Q4 — which delivered the highest-pipeline quarter on record.
+28% pipeline spike attributed to ABM
⏱️
Deal Velocity Paradox: Enterprise Closes 22 Days Faster
Counter-intuitively, Enterprise deals (avg ACV $58K) were closing 22 days faster than Mid-Market deals ($24K ACV). Data drilling revealed the reason: Enterprise prospects came in via ABM, had exec-level buy-in from the first touchpoint, and had already validated budget. Mid-Market deals stalled due to internal budget approval cycles. Recommendation: introduce mid-funnel budget confirmation trigger for Mid-Market segment, and build a champion nurture track for internal stakeholders. Expected to reduce Mid-Market velocity by 15 days.
Enterprise closes 22 days faster than Mid-Market
🤖
Automation Eliminated 40 Hours/Month of Manual Work
Before the ETL + Power BI automation, the analytics team spent 40+ hours per month manually pulling reports from 6 different systems, reconciling data inconsistencies, and building Excel pivot tables. The automated pipeline eliminated this entirely — dashboards refresh every 4 hours with zero manual intervention. This freed the analytics team to focus on strategic work: model building, A/B test analysis, and deeper cohort studies. The time savings alone were valued at ~$28,000/year in productivity reclaimed.
40 hrs/mo saved · ~$28K/yr productivity gain
💰
Referral: Zero-Cost Channel with 31% Close Rate
Referrals were found to have the highest close rate (31%) at zero CPL — yet were receiving zero formal nurturing or enablement. By simply creating a structured referral programme with email follow-up sequences and partner co-marketing assets, the team projected a 40% increase in referral MQL volume. The pipeline model showed that increasing referrals from 6% to just 10% of total MQL mix would save $180K in paid acquisition costs annually while maintaining the same closed revenue output.
31% close rate · $0 CPL · Highest quality source
Pipeline forecasting accuracy improved to ±12% after implementing dimensional data modelling — previously it was ±35%, making Q-end calls unreliable. Leadership can now plan headcount and capacity with confidence.
LinkedIn Ads ROAS grew from 2.8x in Q1 to 4.2x in Q4 as audience targeting was iteratively refined using closed-won data fed back into LinkedIn Campaign Manager — a closed-loop optimisation cycle enabled by the unified pipeline model.
Seasonal pattern detected: MQL volume dips 8-10% in March and August consistently. Pre-emptive budget front-loading in Feb and Jul can smooth the pipeline and prevent the Q-end scramble seen in prior years.
SMB segment disproportionately drains SDR resources — 30% of MQL volume but only 16% of closed revenue. Recommendation: shift SMB to a product-led or self-serve motion, freeing SDRs for Enterprise and Mid-Market where ROI is 3.1x higher per hour of effort.

Revenue Intelligence · FY 2024

Biggest Funnel Drop-Off: MQL → SQL
Out of 3,842 MQLs generated, only 946 converted to SQLs — a 75.3% loss. Deep-dive revealed two root causes: SDR bandwidth bottlenecks during peak campaign periods, and ICP misalignment in paid channels where broad targeting brought in low-fit leads. The fix: tightening the lead scoring threshold from 50 to 65, reducing inbound volume by 18% but increasing SQL conversion rate from 24.7% to 31.2% within 90 days.
→ Action: Raise scoring threshold + SDR capacity audit
41%
Organic Search: Highest Revenue-Per-Lead Channel
Organic search contributes 34% of MQL volume but accounts for 41% of closed revenue — the highest revenue-per-MQL of all channels at $1,380 per lead. Compared to LinkedIn Ads at $672/lead-to-revenue, organic delivers 2x the downstream value per acquisition. This insight directly informed budget reallocation: SEO investment increased 35% in Q4, with content targeting bottom-of-funnel intent keywords that were previously underserved.
→ Action: 35% SEO budget increase in Q4
28%
ABM Campaign Drove 28% Pipeline Spike in Q3
Launching a targeted Account-Based Marketing campaign in Q3 focused on 150 named Enterprise accounts produced a 28% month-over-month pipeline spike — the single largest growth event of the year. Enterprise close rate increased 6 percentage points within 60 days, from 28% to 34%. The key was aligning paid LinkedIn outreach, personalised email sequences, and direct sales outreach to the same account list simultaneously — a cross-channel coordination first for the team.
→ Action: Scale ABM to 300 named accounts in FY25
22 days
Enterprise Closes 22 Days Faster Than Mid-Market
Despite higher ACV ($58K vs $24K), Enterprise deals close an average of 22 days faster than Mid-Market. Analysis traced this to executive-level engagement earlier in the cycle and pre-qualified intent signals (pricing page visits, webinar attendance) that are more common in Enterprise buyers. This finding reshaped SDR prioritisation rules: Enterprise prospects with score ≥80 are now fast-tracked to AE within 4 business hours instead of 48.
→ Action: Fast-track Enterprise 80+ score to AE in 4hrs
18.3%
Close Rate Dipped Despite Pipeline Growth
Overall close rate declined 1.2 percentage points year-over-year even as pipeline volume grew 31%. Root cause analysis using cohort data revealed that the volume growth came disproportionately from lower-quality SMB leads (close rate 11%) diluting the blended average. Mid-Market and Enterprise close rates both improved. Recommendation: implement pipeline quality scoring at the opportunity stage, not just at MQL, to give leadership a more accurate forecast signal.
→ Action: Add opportunity-stage quality scoring to CRM
40hrs
Automated Reporting Freed Strategic Capacity
Before automation, the analytics team spent 40+ hours per month manually pulling data from 6 platforms, reconciling discrepancies, formatting reports, and distributing to 12 stakeholders via email attachments. After deploying the ETL pipeline and Power BI dashboards with scheduled refresh, this dropped to near zero. The reclaimed capacity was reinvested into building the attribution model and lead scoring projects — work that directly contributed to the 18% pipeline recovery outcome.
→ Outcome: Freed 40hrs/mo → reinvested in strategic analytics
