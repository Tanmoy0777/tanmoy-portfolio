🎯 Project 2 of 4 — Multi-Touch Attribution Modelling Framework
Traditional last-touch attribution was giving misleading signals about which channels were truly driving revenue — causing the marketing team to over-invest in bottom-funnel retargeting and under-fund the top-funnel touchpoints that actually initiate and accelerate deals. This project built a full multi-touch attribution framework in SQL and Power BI that compares five attribution models (Linear, Time-Decay, First Touch, Last Touch, and a Data-Driven model based on Shapley values) across 8 marketing channels and $2.4M in closed revenue. The goal: understand the true contribution of each touchpoint so budget allocation decisions are driven by actual revenue impact, not vanity metrics.

🔬 Deep Analysis, Findings & Business Impact
How multi-touch attribution reshaped budget allocation decisions and uncovered $400K+ in misattributed marketing investment
💼
LinkedIn Was Getting 18% Less Credit Than It Deserved
Under last-touch attribution, LinkedIn was attributed only 10% of revenue — causing it to appear as a poor ROI channel (ROAS: 1.8x). The data-driven model revealed LinkedIn actually contributed 28% of closed revenue because it dominated the awareness and consideration stages — prospects almost always had a LinkedIn touchpoint within the first 2 interactions. Correcting this attribution increased LinkedIn's effective ROAS to 4.2x, justifying a budget increase that was previously being rejected by finance.
+18pp credit uplift · ROAS 1.8x → 4.2x
📧
Email Nurture: The Most Undervalued Channel in the Stack
Email nurture sequences showed the most dramatic under-crediting of any channel — only 4% credit in last-touch but 22% of assisted conversions in the data-driven model. The analysis showed email was consistently the 4th or 5th touchpoint in high-value deals, keeping prospects engaged between major touchpoints and preventing funnel leakage. With a CPL of just $28, email has the highest revenue-per-dollar-spent of any channel in the entire mix. This triggered a complete rebuild of email nurture sequences and a 3x increase in email cadence for mid-funnel prospects.
22% assisted conversions · $28 CPL · Highest efficiency
🎓
Webinars: 4.7x ROAS — The Surprise Performer
Linear attribution assigned webinars only a 2.1x ROAS (making them look breakeven). Data-driven model revealed 4.7x ROAS — because webinar attendees were 2.3x more likely to close and had an average deal size 31% higher than non-attendees. The key insight: webinar attendance is an ultra-strong intent signal that acts as a middle-of-funnel accelerator, not just a brand awareness play. Recommendation: gate premium webinar content behind a qualification form to capture intent signals and auto-trigger SDR follow-up within 2 hours of attendance.
4.7x ROAS · 2.3x close lift for attendees
🛣️
Most Profitable Buyer Journey: LinkedIn → Organic → Webinar → Email → Close
Journey path analysis of 164 closed deals revealed the highest-value conversion path generates an average deal size of $48K per closed deal. The sequence: LinkedIn ad creates awareness, organic search validates the brand, a webinar triggers serious consideration, and a targeted email sequence closes the deal. Deals following this path closed 31 days faster than average and had a 92% retention rate at 90 days. This journey pattern is now the template for all enterprise campaign design.
$48K avg deal · 31 days faster close
📊
Time-Decay Model Revealed Q4 Over-Spending on Retargeting
The time-decay model analysis showed that retargeting campaigns in Q4 were receiving disproportionately high credit due to recency bias — contributing to a 35% budget over-allocation to bottom-funnel retargeting. When modelled against actual revenue impact, the incremental value of additional retargeting spend above a $15K/month threshold was near zero. Reallocating $120K from Q4 retargeting to Q1 brand-awareness campaigns was projected to generate 22% more pipeline by expanding the top of funnel.
$120K budget reallocation recommended
🔄
Google PPC: High Volume, Declining Quality Signal
Google PPC contributes 22% of MQL volume (2nd highest) but showed a steady ROAS decline from 3.2x in Q1 to 3.1x in Q4 despite increased spend — suggesting audience saturation and keyword competition erosion. Competitor analysis revealed 3 new entrants bidding on the same high-intent keywords, pushing CPCs up 28% YoY. Recommendation: shift Google PPC strategy from broad keyword coverage to ultra-specific long-tail intent queries, and introduce dayparting rules to concentrate spend in high-conversion time windows (Tue-Thu, 9am-2pm).
ROAS erosion detected · CPC up 28% YoY
Cross-channel synergy effect quantified: Deals with 5+ touchpoints across 3+ different channels had a 2.8x higher close rate than single-channel journeys — proving that integrated multi-channel campaigns significantly outperform channel-siloed approaches.
First-touch attribution overvalues events (12% credit vs 9% in data-driven) because events are often the first contact but rarely the primary conversion driver. This insight corrected over-investment in event sponsorships that weren't converting.
Referral channel ROI is infinite — zero CPL with a 31% close rate and $62K average deal size. With formal referral programme infrastructure, projecting 2x current referral volume within 2 quarters, representing $500K+ in zero-cost pipeline.
Attribution model choice affects budget by ±40%: Switching from last-touch to data-driven shifts LinkedIn allocation from 10% to 28% and email from 4% to 22% — a dramatic rebalancing that changes the entire go-to-market investment strategy.

LinkedIn Ads: The Most Under-Credited Channel
Last-touch attribution assigned LinkedIn only 10% revenue credit, but the data-driven model revealed it deserves 28% — an 18 percentage point difference. LinkedIn's true role is top-of-funnel qualification: it introduces the brand to decision-makers who then convert via organic search or direct. Correcting this misattribution led to a $42K budget increase for LinkedIn in Q4, reversing a planned cut that would have eliminated the company's most efficient Enterprise lead source.
→ Action: Reversed planned LinkedIn cut, added $42K budget
22%
Email Nurture: The Silent Revenue Driver
Email nurture sequences drove 22% of assisted conversions in the data-driven model — yet received only 8% credit in last-touch analysis. The discrepancy exists because nurture emails rarely serve as the final touchpoint, but they consistently appear 2-3 steps before close. Prospects who engaged with 3+ nurture emails converted at 19.4% vs 6.1% for those who didn't — a 3.2x lift. This justified a full rebuild of the email nurture architecture with 8 new automated sequences targeting different intent stages.
→ Action: 8 new nurture sequences built, +3.2x conversion lift
4.7x
Webinars: Highest ROAS in Data-Driven Model
Webinars show 4.7x ROAS in the data-driven model — more than double their 2.1x ROAS in the linear model. The key insight: webinar attendees are self-selected high-intent prospects. Among all channel touchpoints, webinar attendance had the strongest single-event predictive power for conversion. Prospects who attended a webinar closed at 12.4% vs 4.1% for those who didn't. Recommendation: increase webinar frequency from monthly to bi-weekly and create dedicated post-webinar SDR sequences for attendees.
→ Action: Bi-weekly webinar cadence + dedicated SDR follow-up
6.3
Average Deal Requires 6.3 Touchpoints to Close
Mapping 164 closed deals revealed the average buyer journey contains 6.3 marketing touchpoints before converting. The most common pattern: LinkedIn Ad → Organic Search → Content Download → Email Nurture (x2) → Demo Request. This "golden path" appears in 31% of Enterprise deals. Shorter journeys (<4 touchpoints) have a 22% close rate; longer journeys (7+) have 31% — suggesting that nurtured, multi-touch buyers are genuinely higher quality, not just "slow." Implication: never cut mid-funnel touchpoints to reduce cost.
→ Insight: Protect mid-funnel investment — it drives close rate
$180K
Budget Reallocation Based on Model Findings
The attribution model directly informed a $180K Q4 budget reallocation. Funds were moved from Display Advertising (high volume, low data-driven attribution value) to LinkedIn Ads, Email infrastructure, and Webinar production. Google PPC budget was reduced 12% but targeting was tightened to high-intent keywords only, actually improving its ROAS from 3.2x to 3.4x while spending less. This validated the principle: attribution accuracy enables smarter spend, not just more spend.
→ Result: Blended ROAS improved 0.4x on same total budget
3.2x
Model Choice Changes Strategic Decisions Dramatically
Comparing all three models on the same data revealed that the choice of attribution model can change perceived channel ROI by up to 250%. Display Advertising looks profitable under last-touch (it sometimes captures the final click) but shows near-zero value in the data-driven model. The business was previously over-investing in display by $28K/quarter based on last-touch reporting. Switching to the data-driven model as the primary decision-making framework is estimated to save $112K annually in misallocated spend.
→ Saved est. $112K/yr in misallocated display spend
Core Finding
Last-touch attribution systematically under-credits awareness and nurture channels by 40-60%, leading to budget cuts that destroy pipeline quality over time.
Strategic Recommendation
Adopt data-driven attribution as primary model for budget decisions. Keep last-touch as a secondary signal for SDR prioritisation only.
Business Outcome
Blended ROAS improved from 2.8x to 3.2x year-over-year on the same total marketing budget — attributable directly to smarter channel allocation from this model.
