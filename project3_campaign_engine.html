🔬 Deep Analysis, Findings & Business Impact
How automating campaign analytics cut decision lag by 80% and drove 342% ROI through data-driven optimisation cycles
⚡
Decision Latency Reduced from 5 Days to Under 24 Hours
Before automation, the campaign reporting cycle looked like this: data exported manually from 6 platforms (Mon) → consolidated in Excel (Tue-Wed) → reconciliation & QA (Thu) → report built and reviewed (Fri) → decisions made (following Mon). By that time, a failing campaign had burned 7 days of budget. After automation: data is ingested, transformed, and available in Power BI by 6am daily. The first week alone identified a Google Display campaign with 0.28% CTR and $312 CPL that was immediately paused, saving $14,000 in the following month.
5 days → <24 hrs · 80% latency reduction
📉
Google PPC CPL Fell 22% via Automated Bid Adjustments
By connecting funnel conversion data (MQL→SQL rates) back to the ETL pipeline and building automated bid adjustment rules, the system could increase bids on keywords that produced high-quality leads and reduce bids on high-volume but low-quality queries. Within 60 days of implementation, Google PPC CPL dropped from $240 to $187 — a 22% reduction — while maintaining MQL volume. The saving: $53 per lead across 692 Google-sourced leads = $36,676 in acquisition cost savings per year from one channel alone.
CPL $240 → $187 · $36K saved/year on PPC alone
🕐
Dayparting Analysis: 68% of Conversions in 6-Hour Window
Hourly conversion data analysis revealed that 68% of form fills and demo requests occurred between 9am–3pm Tuesday to Thursday (US Eastern). Yet campaigns were spending budget uniformly across all hours and days. Implementing dayparting rules — concentrating 80% of budget in high-conversion windows — increased overall conversion rate by 14% while reducing wasted impressions by 31%. This was only discoverable because the ETL pipeline could process hourly granularity data that Excel-based reporting couldn't handle at scale.
+14% conv rate via dayparting · -31% waste
📊
ROI Trend: 165% → 342% in 12 Months via Continuous Optimisation
The 12-month ROI trend chart shows blended campaign ROI growing from 165% in January to 342% in December — an improvement driven entirely by the automation engine's ability to quickly identify underperformers and reallocate budget to high-performing channels. Each monthly optimisation cycle captured learnings and codified them as rules in the system. The compound effect of 12 months of data-driven iterations produced a 107% improvement in blended ROI — equivalent to generating the same revenue with 52% less ad spend compared to the starting baseline.
165% → 342% ROI · +107pp over 12 months
🎯
Webinar Campaigns: Highest CTR (18.5%) and Fastest Close Cycle
Webinar registration campaigns consistently outperformed all other formats with an 18.5% CTR (vs 0.58% for LinkedIn display) because the "register for free" CTA removes purchase friction entirely. The pipeline data showed webinar leads closed 28 days faster than cold leads and at a 12.4% conversion rate — 2.6x the base rate. The insight: webinars are the most efficient top-funnel-to-close pathway in the entire channel mix. Budget was reallocated from brand awareness display to webinar promotion in Q4, resulting in a 19% improvement in Q4 close rate.
18.5% CTR · 12.4% conv rate · 28d faster close
💸
Display Advertising: Lowest ROI, Highest Waste Identified
Display advertising showed a 0.28% CTR, $312 CPL, and 0.9% conversion rate — placing it dead last in efficiency. Despite this, it represented 18% of total ad budget due to historical inertia. The automation system flagged it as a chronic underperformer within the first week. Pausing display campaigns and redistributing budget to LinkedIn and webinar channels immediately improved blended CPL by $31 and overall pipeline quality score. Display was retained only for retargeting known visitors at a capped $8K/month — a 73% budget reduction.
Display budget cut 73% · CPL improved $31
30% process efficiency gain from ETL automation — not just in reporting time, but in the full campaign decision cycle. Marketing team now operates on weekly optimisation sprints instead of monthly review cycles, compounding improvements faster.
Data quality issues found and fixed: Initial ETL build discovered 12% of CRM lead records had duplicate entries caused by form fill errors, silently inflating MQL counts. Deduplication logic in the pipeline corrected historical data and prevented future inflation, changing the true MQL→SQL conversion rate from 28% (inflated) to 24.7% (accurate).
Email campaigns showed the highest ROI multiplier at 11.8x in Q4 — growing consistently each quarter as email list quality improved through lead scoring integration. Zero wasted impressions, ultra-low CPL of $28, and direct personalisation capability make email the most scalable channel in the stack.
Facebook retargeting showed diminishing returns above $15K/month — incremental conversions plateaued while cost increased linearly. The automation system detected this inflection point 3 months earlier than manual analysis would have, saving approximately $22K in over-spend.

Campaign ROI grew from 250% to 342% over 12 months — not because budgets increased, but because the team could now make data-driven pivots within 24 hours instead of waiting 5 days for manual reports. The most impactful example: a LinkedIn campaign showing declining CPL in real-time was paused and budget was redirected to a high-performing Google Search campaign within the same day, saving an estimated $18K in wasted spend. Speed-to-insight is a direct revenue driver.

Google PPC CPL Reduced Through Automated Bid Rules
After connecting live funnel conversion data to Google Ads bid strategies via the ETL pipeline, automated bid adjustment rules were introduced: keywords with funnel conversion rates below 2% had bids reduced 20%; keywords with rates above 5% received 15% bid increases. This dynamic adjustment — impossible with manual weekly reporting — reduced blended Google PPC CPL from $240 to $187 (a 22% reduction) while maintaining lead volume. The system now adjusts bids daily based on prior 7-day funnel data.
→ CPL dropped $240 → $187 via automated bid rules

Previously, the team compiled campaign reports manually: pulling exports from 6 platforms, cleaning data in Excel, reconciling naming conventions, building charts, and distributing via email. The process took one analyst ~10 hours per week. After ETL automation, every metric — CTR, CPL, conversion rate, pipeline contribution — refreshes automatically every 4 hours. Leadership now reviews live dashboards in their morning standup instead of waiting for Friday report emails. Cultural shift: data became a daily tool, not a weekly document.

Webinar Campaigns: Highest ROI — Scaling Recommended
The automation engine surfaced that the Webinar campaign series was generating 478% ROI — the highest of any channel — yet was receiving only 8% of total marketing budget. This was invisible in manual reporting because webinar data lived in a separate event platform not connected to the CRM. After the ETL integration, this channel's true contribution became measurable for the first time. Budget allocation to webinars increased from 8% to 18% in Q4. Expected FY25 webinar revenue contribution: $420K, up from $264K.
→ Webinar budget doubled; projected +$156K revenue FY25
3
Three Underperforming Channels Identified & Restructured
Automated performance benchmarking flagged 3 campaigns as persistent underperformers: Display Advertising (CPL $312, ROI 89%), Facebook Prospecting (CPL $245, ROI 142%), and Twitter/X (CPL $380, ROI 71%). Combined, these channels consumed $68K/quarter with below-benchmark returns. Rather than cutting them entirely, the findings drove a creative refresh and audience retargeting strategy. Post-restructure: Display CPL dropped to $195, Facebook to $198. Twitter was discontinued. Freed $22K/quarter reinvested into LinkedIn and webinars.
→ Freed $22K/qtr from underperformers → reinvested strategically
+30%
Process Efficiency Gain Across Marketing Operations
The 30% process efficiency gain isn't just about saved hours — it represents a fundamental shift in how marketing operates. Pre-automation: reactive analysis of past performance. Post-automation: proactive optimisation of live performance. The team now runs weekly A/B tests on ad creative, landing pages, and email subject lines — analyses that simply weren't possible when data was only available weekly. Three creative A/B tests in Q3 alone improved conversion rates by a combined 14%, adding ~$90K to pipeline that quarter.
→ A/B testing programme added ~$90K pipeline in Q3
Architectural Insight
Dimensional data modelling (star schema) was critical — it reduced query time from 45 seconds to under 3 seconds and enabled cross-source joins that were previously impossible in flat files.
Trend Identified
Channel performance shows strong seasonality: LinkedIn peaks in Q1/Q3 (budget cycles), webinars peak in Q2/Q4. Scheduling campaigns to match these windows improves ROAS by ~18%.
System Reliability
99.8% pipeline uptime over 12 months with zero manual interventions required. The 0.2% downtime was caused by a single API rate limit breach, now resolved with automated throttling.
