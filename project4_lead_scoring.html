Lead Quality Scoring & Cohort Analysis System
A prospect scoring model and cohort analysis framework built to solve a critical RevOps problem: the sales team was spending equal time on leads that would never close and leads that were ready to buy. This project developed a 15-signal weighted scoring algorithm combining firmographic data (job title, company size, industry), behavioural signals (website visits, content downloads, email engagement), and intent signals (pricing page visits, demo requests, webinar attendance). Leads are scored 0–100 and routed to three tiers: Cold (<40), Warm (41–70), and Hot (71–100). Cohort analysis tracks conversion rates, pipeline velocity, and retention by buyer segment across 6-month windows. The model, deployed in July 2024, recovered 18% additional qualified pipeline within 60 days.

🔬 Project 4 of 4 — Lead Quality Scoring Model & Cohort Analysis
Without a formal lead scoring system, the sales team was spending equal time on every lead in the queue — wasting SDR hours on cold prospects while high-intent enterprise leads sat unattended. This project built a 15-signal, multi-dimensional lead scoring model from scratch using behavioural, firmographic, and intent data from Marketo, GA4, and CRM. The model assigns a 0-100 quality score to every inbound lead in real time, automatically routes them to the appropriate sales motion (self-serve, inside sales, or strategic enterprise), and triggers personalised nurture sequences based on score tier. The accompanying cohort analysis tracks how different buyer segments retain, convert, and generate lifetime value over 6-month windows — enabling proactive intervention before churn occurs.

🔬 Deep Analysis, Findings & Business Impact
How a data-driven lead scoring model recovered 18% qualified pipeline, improved SDR efficiency by 3x, and unlocked predictive revenue forecasting
🏆
Score 80+ Leads Convert at 34.2% — 16x Better Than Cold Leads
The most powerful validation of the scoring model: leads scoring 81-100 convert at a 34.2% rate while leads scoring 0-20 convert at just 2.1% — a 16.3x difference. Before scoring, SDRs spent equal time on every lead. After implementation, SDR time allocation shifted to 80% focus on score 70+ leads, increasing average conversion rate per SDR from 8.4% to 22.7% — effectively tripling SDR productivity without hiring anyone. The result: same headcount, 2.7x more closed revenue per SDR per quarter.
34.2% conv rate · 16x better than cold · 3x SDR productivity
🔍
3 Drop-Off Points Found — 18% Pipeline Recovered in 60 Days
Deep funnel analysis combined with the scoring model identified 3 specific points where high-scoring leads (70+) were going cold without any follow-up: (1) leads who attended a webinar but didn't receive an SDR call within 48 hours had a 67% probability of going cold, (2) enterprise leads stuck in "SQL" stage for 14+ days with no activity had a 78% churn probability, and (3) mid-market leads who visited the pricing page 3+ times without a demo request needed a direct outbound trigger. Automated workflows for each of these patterns recovered 692 leads and $750K in pipeline within 60 days.
+18% pipeline · $750K recovered · 3 patterns fixed
🏢
Enterprise Segment: 68% 6-Month Retention vs 24% for SMB
Cohort retention analysis revealed a stark difference in long-term value by segment. Enterprise cohorts retained 68% of initial pipeline activity at 6 months, Mid-Market retained 48%, and SMB retained only 24%. This means SMB leads require 2.8x more nurturing investment to generate the same 6-month pipeline contribution as Enterprise. Factoring in ACV (Enterprise $58K vs SMB $8K), the lifetime value gap between a single Enterprise lead and SMB lead is 12.8x — justifying aggressive resource reallocation toward Enterprise acquisition.
Enterprise LTV = 12.8x SMB · 68% vs 24% retention
⚡
"Demo Request" Signal: Strongest Single Conversion Predictor
Signal-level analysis across all 15 scoring factors revealed that "Demo Request" (11 points) was the single strongest predictor of conversion — leads with this signal converted at 41.2%, nearly 5x the base rate. However, the model also found that "Webinar Attendance + Pricing Page Visit" as a combined signal (even without a formal demo request) predicted conversion at 38.7% — nearly as strongly. This combination trigger was added as a hidden high-intent flag that auto-pushes leads to the SDR queue without requiring a formal demo request, capturing demand that was previously being missed.
41.2% conv on demo req · 38.7% on webinar+pricing combo
📈
Model Iterated 5 Times — Each Iteration Improved Precision by 4-8%
The scoring model wasn't built once and left static — it was iterated 5 times over FY 2024 as new conversion data validated or disproved signal assumptions. Key changes: webinar signal weight increased from 6 to 9 points after Q3 data showed webinar attendees converting at 12.4%; pricing page visit added as a new signal in November after detecting it in high-converting journey paths; company size recalibrated to distinguish 200-500 employee companies as "high-fit" (previously lumped with SMB). Each iteration directly improved model accuracy and downstream conversion rates.
5 iterations · Accuracy 68% → 78.4% over FY 2024
🎯
Firmographic Signals: 42% of Score Weight, Highest Business Value
Of the 15 scoring signals, firmographic data (company size, industry, job title, technology stack) contributes 42% of total score weight and has the highest correlation with deal size and close probability. The insight: knowing WHO the lead is matters more than what they've clicked. A VP of IT at a 500-person financial services company with a score of 65 is a better sales investment than a Marketing Manager at a 20-person startup with a score of 85. The model accounts for this by weighting firmographic signals most heavily, ensuring high-value prospects aren't underscored due to early-stage behavioural activity.
42% firmographic weight · Highest deal size correlation
Seasonal cohort pattern identified: Leads acquired in September–October have consistently 22% higher 6-month conversion rates than leads acquired in January–February — driven by fiscal year-end budget spend. This intelligence now drives media spend front-loading into Q3/Q4.
Score-based routing eliminated 34% of unqualified leads from the sales queue entirely — routing them instead to automated nurture tracks. SDR team reported a 44% reduction in time spent on dead-end calls in the 6 months post-implementation, dramatically improving morale and quota attainment.
Technology stack signal added late in FY 2024: Leads from companies using competitive platforms (e.g. legacy LMS vendors) showed 28% higher conversion when targeted with specific competitive displacement messaging. This signal is now part of the ABM targeting layer.
Model predicted $1.1M in Q1 2025 pipeline based on lead quality distribution and historical conversion rates — within 8% of actual Q1 results. This validated the model as a reliable forecasting tool, enabling the CFO to plan with greater confidence and reduce conservative cash-flow buffers.

Score 80+ Leads Convert at 4.7x the Base Rate
The most important finding from the scoring model: leads with a score of 80 or above convert to closed won at 34.2% — compared to 7.3% for the full lead base. That's a 4.7x difference. These 439 leads (11.4% of the total) generated 52% of all closed revenue. Recognising this concentration, routing rules were updated: any Score 80+ lead is now automatically assigned to an AE within 4 business hours and receives a personalised outreach sequence — eliminating the previous 48-hour generic SDR queue that was causing high-value leads to go cold.
→ Score 80+ fast-tracked to AE in 4hrs, not 48hrs
+18%
18% Pipeline Recovered: The 3 Drop-Off Points Fixed
Before the scoring model, 18% of high-quality leads were being lost in three specific points: (1) Score 70-79 leads sat in the general queue for 6+ days before SDR contact; (2) Leads who attended webinars but didn't fill a form were not captured in CRM; (3) Enterprise leads from companies already in the CRM were being treated as new MQLs, duplicating effort. After fixing all three: a new mid-tier routing rule for Score 70-79, webinar attendance automatically triggering a CRM record, and account deduplication logic — 18% more qualified pipeline was recovered within 60 days.
→ 3 systemic fixes recovered 18% qualified pipeline in 60 days
42%
Firmographic Signals: The Strongest Predictors
Firmographic signals (job title, company size, industry vertical) collectively account for 42% of total score weight and proved to be the strongest predictors of eventual close. Specifically: Director-level and above job titles increase close probability by 2.8x. Companies with 200-2000 employees (Mid-Market to Enterprise) show 3.1x higher conversion than smaller firms. SaaS, Financial Services, and Healthcare industries convert at nearly double the rate of other verticals. These insights were used to refine not just scoring, but also campaign targeting parameters in LinkedIn and Google Ads.
→ ICP refinement improved paid campaign targeting by 28%

Enterprise Cohort Retains 68% at Month 6 vs SMB's 24%
Cohort retention analysis revealed a stark divide: Enterprise customers retained 68% engagement at month 6 post-acquisition; SMB retained only 24%. This retention gap has direct revenue implications — Enterprise customers have a 3.2x higher LTV despite requiring more acquisition investment. The cohort data supported a strategic recommendation to deliberately reduce SMB volume (high cost, low retention) in favour of Enterprise (higher CAC but dramatically better LTV). This finding became a key input into FY25 go-to-market strategy.
→ GTM shift: reduce SMB volume, concentrate on Enterprise
78.4%
Model Accuracy Improves With Each Iteration
The initial scoring model launched at 64% predictive accuracy — meaning 64% of leads predicted to convert actually did. Through 5 iterative updates (adding the pricing page signal, recalibrating company size bands, increasing webinar weight, lowering the routing threshold, and refining industry vertical values), accuracy improved to 78.4% over 6 months. Each update was validated against a holdout cohort of 200 leads before deployment. The improvement trajectory suggests 85%+ accuracy is achievable with 12 more months of conversion data to train on.
→ 5 model iterations: 64% → 78.4% accuracy in 6 months
3.2x
Email-Engaged Leads Convert at 3.2x Non-Engaged Rate
One of the most actionable cohort findings: leads who opened 3+ marketing emails within a 30-day window converted at 19.4% vs 6.1% for those who didn't engage with email at all — a 3.2x difference. This "email engagement signal" was subsequently added as an 8-point score factor in model v3. More importantly, it revealed that many high-scoring leads were not being included in email sequences because they lacked a direct opt-in. Implementing a soft-opt-in flow from demo request forms added 340 net-new email subscribers per month who now receive nurture sequences.
→ +340 net-new nurture subscribers/month via opt-in fix

Unexpected Finding
